================================================================================
CORE PHYSICS IMPLEMENTATION
================================================================================

1. KEPLER ORBITAL ELEMENTS (6 Parameters)
   ─────────────────────────────────────

   a  = Semi-major axis (AU)              → How far from sun
   e  = Eccentricity (0-1)                → How elliptical
   i  = Inclination (degrees)             → Tilt angle
   Ω  = Longitude of ascending node       → Rotation reference
   ω  = Argument of perihelion            → Closest point angle
   M  = Mean anomaly                      → Time-dependent


2. POSITION CALCULATION (5-Step Process)
   ────────────────────────────────────

   Step 1: Solve Kepler's equation (M = E - e·sin(E))
           Using Newton-Raphson iteration
           E_{n+1} = M + e·sin(E_n)

   Step 2: Calculate true anomaly from eccentric anomaly
           tan(ν/2) = sqrt((1+e)/(1-e)) × tan(E/2)

   Step 3: Calculate distance from sun
           r = a(1-e²)/(1 + e·cos(ν))

   Step 4: Get 3D coordinates in orbital plane
           x = r·cos(ν), y = r·sin(ν), z = 0

   Step 5: Apply 3D rotation transformations
           Rotate by ω, i, then Ω using rotation matrices
           Final position: (X, Y, Z) in inertial frame


3. VELOCITY CALCULATIONS
   ──────────────────────

   Orbital Velocity:       v = √(GM/r)
   Escape Velocity:        v_e = √(2GM/r)
   Circular Orbit Period:  T = 2π√(a³/GM)
   Mean Motion:            n = 2π/T = √(GM/a³)


4. GRAVITATIONAL FORCE
   ───────────────────

   Newton's Law:    F = G·m₁·m₂/r²
   
   Constants:
   - G = 6.67430 × 10⁻¹¹ m³/(kg·s²)  [CODATA 2018]
   - AU = 1.496 × 10¹¹ m
   - M☉ = 1.989 × 10³⁰ kg
   - M⊕ = 5.972 × 10²⁴ kg


5. ADVANCED PHYSICS
   ────────────────

   Orbital Resonance:  T₁/T₂ = p/q (integer ratio)
   Tidal Force:        ΔF = 2·G·M·R/r³
   Hill Sphere:        d = a·(m/M)^(1/3)
   Roche Limit:        d = 2.456·R·(M₁/M₂)^(1/3)

CONTROL PANEL PARAMETERS (⚙️ For Real Experiments)
================================================================================

THREE ADJUSTABLE PARAMETERS:

1. 🌞 Sun Mass Factor (0.5 - 2.5×)
   ─────────────────────────────────
   Default: 1.0
   
   Changes:
   - All orbital velocities proportionally: v ∝ √M
   - Orbital periods by Kepler's law: T ∝ √(1/M)
   - All planets' motion recalculates
   - Physics Lab shows new velocities
   
   Try: Set to 2.0 → Planets orbit faster
        Set to 0.5 → Planets orbit slower


2. 🪐 Gravity Factor (0.2 - 2.0×)
   ──────────────────────────────
   Default: 1.0
   
   Changes:
   - Scales gravitational constant (G)
   - All distances maintained, forces scale
   - Orbital periods recalculate by: T ∝ √(1/G)
   - Escape velocities scale: v_e ∝ √G
   
   Try: 2.0 = "Heavy gravity universe"
        0.5 = "Light gravity universe"


3. ⏱️ Simulation Speed (0 - 3.0×)
   ──────────────────────────
   Default: 1.0
   
   Changes:
   - Time acceleration factor only (no physics change)
   - 1.0 = Real-time (1 day per second)
   - 10.0 = 1 year per day simulation
   - 0.0 = Pause
   
   Try: 10 = Watch orbital motion quickly

================================================================================
EXAMPLE CALCULATIONS (Real Values)
================================================================================

EXAMPLE 1: Earth's Orbital Velocity
────────────────────────────────────
Formula: v = √(GM/r)

Given:
- G = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ m³/(kg·s²)
- M = 1.989 × 10³⁰ kg (Sun)
- r = 1.496 × 10¹¹ m (1 AU)

Calculate:
v = √((6.674 × 10⁻¹¹) × (1.989 × 10³⁰) / (1.496 × 10¹¹))
v = √(1.327 × 10²⁰ / 1.496 × 10¹¹)
v = √(8.867 × 10⁸)
v ≈ 29,785 m/s = 29.79 km/s ✓


EXAMPLE 2: Sun-Earth Gravitational Force
──────────────────────────────────────────
Formula: F = G·m₁·m₂/r²

Given:
- G = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹
- m₁ = 1.989 × 10³⁰ kg (Sun)
- m₂ = 5.972 × 10²⁴ kg (Earth)
- r = 1.496 × 10¹¹ m

Calculate:
F = (6.674 × 10⁻¹¹) × (1.989 × 10³⁰) × (5.972 × 10²⁴) / (1.496 × 10¹¹)²
F = (7.926 × 10⁴⁴) / (2.238 × 10²²)
F ≈ 3.54 × 10²² N ✓


EXAMPLE 3: Earth Escape Velocity
─────────────────────────────────
Formula: v_e = √(2GM/r)

Given:
- G = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹
- M = 5.972 × 10²⁴ kg (Earth)
- r = 6.371 × 10⁶ m (Earth radius)

Calculate:
v_e = √((2 × 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹) × (5.972 × 10²⁴) / (6.371 × 10⁶))
v_e = √(7.974 × 10¹⁴ / 6.371 × 10⁶)
v_e = √(1.251 × 10⁸)
v_e ≈ 11,186 m/s = 11.19 km/s ✓
